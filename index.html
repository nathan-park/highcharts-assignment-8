Employment In the United States

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, Employment status of the civilian noninstitutional population, 1944 to date

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, Employment status of the civilian noninstitutional population, 1944 to date

by Nathan Park

Employment is one of the most important issues among the American people. As our labor force continues to grow, it is important that more jobs are available so that we are not left with an overwhelming population of the unemployed. The percentage of unemployed civilians varies depending on the state of the economy, but over the past half decade we have seen a gradual decrease in the percentage of unemployed population to the labor force population. This may look to be a good sign, but other factors can determine the unemployment rate. However, in looking at the total population of the labor force, the unemployment rate as gone down significantly in relation to the growth of the total labor force, suggesting that while more people are entering the labor force, even more are able to find jobs.
